What is unbanked?

A household is considered "unbanked" when no one in that household had a checking or savings account at a bank or credit union.

What is underbanked?

A household is considered "underbanked" when the household was banked but in the past 12 months used at least one of the following non-bank transaction or credit products or services that are disproportionately used by unbanked households to meet their transaction and credit needs:

  • Money orders, check cashing, or international remittances (i.e., non-bank transactions)
  • Rent-to-own services or payday, pawn shop, tax refund anticipation, or auto title loans (i.e., non-bank credit)

Primary Method of Bank Account Access
from 2013 to 2021

The FDIC National Survey categorizes 6 methods of bank account access:

  • visiting a bank teller;
  • using an ATM or bank kiosk;
  • calling the bank (i.e., telephone banking);
  • using a computer or tablet (i.e., online banking);
  • using an app, text messaging, or internet browser on a mobile phone (i.e., mobile banking);
  • or using some other method (i.e., other)
